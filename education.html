Core concepts behind the test

Use this as a quick reference while comparing eyes-open and eyes-closed trials in Romberger.

What is balance?

Balance is the body's ability to keep its center of mass over its base of support. It depends on three systems working together: visual input, vestibular input from the inner ear, and proprioception from muscles and joints.

What is loss of balance?

Loss of balance happens when those systems cannot coordinate effectively. You may see larger sway, delayed corrective movements, or sudden instability. Fatigue, sensory deficits, medication effects, and neurologic issues can all contribute.

About the Romberg test

The Romberg test compares stability with eyes open versus eyes closed. If sway increases notably with eyes closed, it suggests stronger reliance on visual input and possible proprioceptive impairment. The test is useful for screening, but by itself is not diagnostic.
